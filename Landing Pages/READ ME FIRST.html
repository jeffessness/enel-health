Read Me First

This folder holds the web pages that invite people to join an Ēnel study. Here is what everything is, in plain language.

What is a landing page?

It is the page someone lands on when they click an ad, an email, or a link about one of our studies. Its job is to explain the study and get them to sign up.

Every study uses three pages, in order:

  • Landing — the first page. It introduces the study and says why it matters.
  • Join — the sign-up page. This is where a person enters to take part.
  • Payoff — the thank-you page they see right after they sign up, telling them what happens next.

How to open them

Double-click any .html file and it opens in your web browser. Start with the index.html inside a set to preview all of that set's pages in one place. If a page looks blank, right-click it and choose Open with → Chrome.
