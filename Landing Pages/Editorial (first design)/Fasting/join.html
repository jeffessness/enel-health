Why this matters

Fasting is loud online and thin on evidence.

Most of what you read is a controlled trial nobody lives in, or a coach with something to sell. This study watches real people follow real protocols in their own lives — mood, energy, sleep, hunger, and focus — and measures each against where they started.

How it works.

What you do
Pick the protocol closest to what you already do, then submit a short daily check-in — mood, energy, sleep, hunger, cravings, and focus.
What we do
We aggregate every check-in, surface the patterns, and publish all of it, including the null results, with participants seeing it first.
What the agent does
The study agent explains the science and helps you match a protocol. It will not give medical advice; if it starts to sound like it would, it points you to a clinician.

Protocols

Choose what matches you.

Select the option closest to what you’re already doing or planning. You can change it later.

12:12 intermittent fasting
Beginner · 14 days
Twelve hours fasting overnight, twelve eating. Most people already do this without realising it.
16:8 intermittent fasting
Beginner · 21 days
A sixteen-hour fast with an eight-hour eating window. The classic pattern.
OMAD (one meal a day)
Intermediate · 14 days
One large, nutritious meal a day; water and electrolytes the rest of the time.
24-hour fast
Intermediate · 7 days
A single 24-hour fast, once or twice a week. Builds metabolic flexibility quickly.
48-hour fast
Advanced · 7 days
A 48-hour water fast, monthly. Deeper autophagy and reset territory.
72-hour fast
Advanced · 7 days
A 72-hour fast, attempted once, for experienced fasters.
Water fast (custom length)
Advanced
A pure water fast of your chosen length. Coordinate with the agent before starting.
Sugar fast
Beginner · 30 days
No added sugar for thirty days; eat normally otherwise. Resets palate and insulin response.
Lemon water fast
Intermediate · 7 days
A seven-day fluid-only protocol — water, lemon, and a pinch of salt.
Keto-fasting hybrid
Intermediate · 21 days
A ketogenic pattern paired with a 16:8 daily window. Easier glucose control while learning to fast.

Before you join.
Fasting can be unsafe for some people. Do not start a fasting protocol if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, have a current or past eating disorder, take medication for diabetes (especially insulin or sulfonylureas), have a chronic medical condition, or are under 18. Talk to your physician first. This study is observational — not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a treatment plan. The study agent will not give medical advice; if it sounds like one, talk to a clinician instead. You can withdraw at any time. Findings are published aggregated and de-identified.
