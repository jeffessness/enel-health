Before you enroll.

What do I actually have to do, and how long does it run?
Pick the protocol closest to what you already take, then spend about a minute a day on a check-in: energy, sleep, recovery, mood, and any side effects. Most protocols run 28 to 56 days. That's the whole commitment — report honestly, and let the numbers build against your own starting point.
Do I keep using my own peptide and dose, or does the study change my protocol?
You keep your own. This is an observational study: we watch what you're already doing, we don't tell you to start, stop, or change a dose. You report it; we measure it against where you began.
Do I need a prescription, and do you supply the peptide or do I use my own?
We don't prescribe, sell, or source anything, and we never will. You report only what you're already using, or planning to use under a physician's supervision. The study has no stake in what you take — that's what makes the read an honest one.
Is it safe, and should I involve my doctor?
Involve your doctor — this is a decision to make with a clinician, not with us. Peptides vary widely in quality, legality, and safety, and many are not cleared by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). Don't take part if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, trying to conceive, under 18, or in active cancer treatment without your oncologist's clearance, and if anything serious comes up, stop and call your physician.
What will I see at the end, and who can see my data?
You see your own result first — your numbers against your baseline — then the cohort report: the effect size, its uncertainty, and the open questions that follow. Your raw data stays yours and is never sold. Anything we publish is combined across members and stripped of names, and founding members keep their data forever.
