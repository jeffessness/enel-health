Questions

Before you enroll.

How do you measure my sleep, and do I need a wearable or device?
No wearable required. You log it yourself each morning: how long it took to fall asleep, how deeply you slept, how often you woke, and how you felt the next day. If you own a sleep tracker you can fold it in, but your own report is enough.
What does the digital sunset actually require each night, and how strict is it?
As strict as the protocol you choose. The gentlest is sixty screen-free minutes before bed; the next is a ninety-minute wind-down with reading, stretching, or journaling; the deepest is a full evening tech detox. You pick the one that fits your life and hold it for the run.
What if I already sleep badly, or already sleep fine?
Both are worth knowing. If you sleep badly, you find out whether the screen-free hour actually moves anything for you. If you sleep fine, your nights become part of the evidence on whether it matters for people who already do — measured against your own baseline either way.
What do I actually have to do, and how long does it run?
Hold your chosen screen-free routine each night, and spend a minute each morning logging how you slept. Protocols run 14 to 30 days. That's it — the sacrifice is the phone, not your time.
What will I see at the end, and who can see my data?
You see your own result first — your sleep against your nights before — then the cohort report: the effect size, its uncertainty, and the open questions. Your raw data stays yours and is never sold; anything published is combined across members and stripped of names, and founding members keep theirs forever.
